A JOURNEY THROUGH TIME

Chess isn't just a game; it's a 1,500-year-old legacy of human intelligence.

Origins (6th Century)

The game began in India as Chaturanga. It simulated ancient warfare with infantry, cavalry, elephants, and chariots.

As it moved through Persia and the Islamic world, it evolved into Shatranj. In the 15th century, Europeans sped up the game, creating the fast-paced modern version we play today.

THE BOARD & EQUIPMENT

The standard for professional chess was set in 1849 with the Staunton Design. Before this, chess pieces were often confusingly different in every region.

The Staunton Standard

Registered by Nathaniel Cooke and endorsed by Howard Staunton, this design is now the global standard for all FIDE tournaments. The Knight's head is inspired by the Elgin Marbles!

Board Orientation

Always place the board so that a light square is in the bottom-right corner for both players. "White on right!"

PIECES & THEIR VALUES

While the goal is to trap the King, understanding the relative power of pieces helps in making strategic trades.

Piece Point Value Role on the Board
Pawn 1 pt The "soul of chess." Used for structure and promotion.
Knight 3 pts The trickster. Jumps over pieces. Deadly in forks.
Bishop 3 pts Long-range diagonal attacker. Strong in open positions.
Rook 5 pts Heavy artillery. Dominates files and ranks.
Queen 9 pts The most powerful piece. A combination of Rook and Bishop.
King Infinity The ultimate objective. Must be kept safe at all costs.

FAMOUS OPENINGS

The first few moves often dictate the entire game. Here are some of the most studied openings in history:

Sicilian Defense (1. e4 c5)
The most popular and high-scoring response to White's 1. e4. It creates an asymmetrical battle.
Ruy Lopez (1. e4 e5 2. Nf3 Nc6 3. Bb5)
One of the oldest and most analyzed openings, named after a Spanish priest.
Queen's Gambit (1. d4 d5 2. c4)
White offers a pawn to gain control of the center. High-level and highly strategic.
King's Indian Defense
A "hypermodern" opening where Black allows White to take the center only to attack it later.

STRATEGY & TACTICS

Tactical Glossary

  • The Fork: A single piece attacks two or more enemy pieces at once.
  • The Pin: A piece is stuck because moving it would lose a more valuable piece behind it.
  • The Skewer: A valuable piece is attacked and forced to move, exposing a piece behind it.
  • Discovered Attack: Moving one piece to unleash an attack from another piece.

CHESS ETIQUETTE

Chess is a gentleman's game. Following these rules shows respect for your opponent and the game.

  • The Handshake: Always shake hands before and after a game.
  • Touch-Move: If you touch a piece, you must move it. If you touch an opponent's piece, you must capture it.
  • "J'adoube": If you only want to adjust a piece, say "I adjust" or "J'adoube" before touching it.
  • Silence: Never talk during a game unless to offer a draw or say "check."

THE IMMORTAL GAME

In 1851, Adolf Anderssen and Lionel Kieseritzky played a game that would be remembered forever.

Anderssen sacrificed his Queen, both Rooks, and a Bishop to force a checkmate with only his remaining minor pieces. It is the perfect example of the "Romantic era" of chess—where creativity and attack were valued above everything else.
