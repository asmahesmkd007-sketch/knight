6. TOURNAMENT MECHANICS & RULES

Phoenix-X is a skill-based platform where users compete in chess. The following rules govern all competitive events:

6.1 Tournament Formats

  1. Knockout (Single Elimination): Players are paired in rounds. The winner advances, and the loser is eliminated.
  2. Swiss System: Players are paired against opponents with similar scores. No one is eliminated until the final round.
  3. Arena: A fast-paced format where players can play as many games as possible within a time limit.

6.2 Match Timing (The Clock)

Every match is played with a countdown timer (e.g., 1 min, 3 min, 5 min). If your clock reaches zero, you lose the game immediately, regardless of the board position (unless the opponent has insufficient material to mate, in which case it is a draw).

6.3 Disconnection Policy

Stable internet is a prerequisite for online chess. Phoenix-X is not responsible for losses caused by your local network failures, device battery issues, or ISP outages.

  • Reconnect Window: If you disconnect, the game remains active. You should try to reconnect as quickly as possible. Your clock will continue to run while you are disconnected.
  • Abandonment: If a player fails to make a move within the first 15 seconds of a match, the game may be automatically cancelled and the player penalized.

6.4 Fair Pairing

Our matchmaking algorithm pairs players based on their IQ Level and historical performance. We strive to provide a balanced competitive environment.

6.5 Prize Pool Calculation

For Paid Tournaments, the prize pool is generated from user entry fees. Phoenix-X deducts a Platform Service Fee (Commission) of approximately 15.1% to cover operational costs, prize management, and tax compliance. The remaining 84.9% is distributed among the winners.

7. FAIR PLAY & ANTI-CHEAT STANDARDS

The integrity of chess is paramount. We employ a multi-layered detection system to ensure a level playing field.

7.1 Definition of Cheating

Cheating includes, but is not limited to:

  • Using chess engines like Stockfish, Komodo, or AlphaZero.
  • Using "opening books" or move databases during a live match.
  • Receiving move suggestions from another person (coaching).
  • Using automated scripts to move pieces (bots).
  • "Sandbagging" (losing intentionally to play against weaker opponents).

7.2 Detection Methods

Our Anti-Cheat system analyzes every move made on the Platform. We check for:

  • Move Accuracy: Correlation with top engine choices.
  • Time Consistency: Uniform delays between moves (suggesting engine input).
  • Browser Behavior: Toggling tabs or losing focus during a match.
  • Performance Spikes: Sudden, unexplained increases in playing strength.

7.3 Consequences of Violation

If our system or moderation team identifies a player as a cheater:

  1. The account will be permanently banned.
  2. All remaining wallet balances (Deposits and Winnings) will be forfeited.
  3. The user's IP and device ID will be blacklisted.
  4. In severe cases, legal action may be initiated for fraudulent gain.

11. APPENDICES - DETAILED PROCEDURES

APPENDIX A: DETAILED CHESS RULES & STANDARDS

Phoenix-X follows the FIDE (International Chess Federation) Laws of Chess, with specific adaptations for the digital environment.

  • The Board: 64 squares, 8x8 grid. White on right.
  • The Pieces: 16 pieces per player. Standard starting position.
  • The King: Moves 1 square. Subject to check and checkmate.
  • The Queen: Moves any distance in any straight line.
  • The Rook: Moves any distance horizontally or vertically.
  • The Bishop: Moves any distance diagonally.
  • The Knight: Moves in an "L" shape. Only piece that jumps.
  • The Pawn: Moves 1 forward (2 on first move). Captures diagonally.
  • Castling: Special move involving King and Rook. Subject to "King safety" rules.
  • En Passant: Special pawn capture immediately after a 2-square move.
  • Promotion: Pawn reaching the last rank becomes a Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.
  • Draws: Stalemate, Threefold Repetition, 50-Move Rule, Insufficient Material.

APPENDIX B: TOURNAMENT PAIRING & MECHANICS

Our pairing system is designed to be transparent and fair. We use a modified Elo-based system adjusted for Blitz and Bullet speeds.

  • Initial Seeding: Players are seeded based on their current IQ Level.
  • Round 1 Pairing: In Knockouts, Top Seed plays Bottom Seed (1 vs 32, 2 vs 31, etc.).
  • Tie-Breaking: In non-knockout formats, ties are broken using Buchholz, Sonneborn-Berger, or cumulative scores.
  • Bye Points: If an odd number of players remain, one player may receive a "Bye" (1 point or advancement).

APPENDIX H: GLOSSARY OF CHESS TERMS

  • Blunder: A very bad move that loses material or the game.
  • Fianchetto: Developing a bishop to the longest diagonal.
  • Gambit: Sacrificing material (usually a pawn) for an advantage.
  • Middlegame: The part of the game between opening and endgame.
  • Notation (Algebraic): The standard method for recording chess moves (e.g., e4, Nf3).

To manage user expectations, the following timeline is standard for all withdrawal requests:

  • Phase 3: Queue Management (30 mins - 2 hours): Requests are processed in the order they were received. Queue position is visible in the Wallet section.
